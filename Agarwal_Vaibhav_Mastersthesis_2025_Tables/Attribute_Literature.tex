\textbf{Decision} & 
Area scenario: Human decisions are considered fairer and trusted more than ADM systems.  \\ 
& Individual scenario: Algorithmic decisions are seen as fairer. \citep{Hobson2021} \\ 
& Ref: \citep{Hobson2021, meier1975representative, Miller2020} \\  
\hline
\textbf{Agency and Accountability} & 
In high-discretion parking cases, participants preferred lower human involvement (25\%). \citep{Horvath2023} \\ 
& Algorithmic decisions with a human-in-the-loop governance mechanism are perceived as more legitimate than mere notice. \citep{Waldman2022} \\  
& Ref: \citep{Grimmelikhuijsen2022, Hobson2021, Horvath2023, Waldman2022, Schaap2023} \\  
\hline
\textbf{Mistakes} & 
When users see an algorithm make mistakes, they prefer the human forecaster. \citep{dietvorst2015algorithm} \\ 
& Successful algorithmic decisions increase trust and support for police decision-making. \citep{Hobson2021} \\  
& Ref: \citep{dietvorst2015algorithm, eubanks_automating_2018, Gritsenko2022Algorithmic, Hobson2021} \\  
\hline
\textbf{Interest}  & 
Bureaucratic proximity matters: Citizens are more likely to trust, accept, and pay higher (tax and data) for sheriffs than the FBI. \citep{Schiff2023} \\ 
& Institutional trust is a significant predictor of algorithm acceptance. \citep{Wenzelburger2022} \\  
& Ref: \citep{Wenzelburger2022, Schiff2023, Miller2020} \\  
\hline
\textbf{Transparency} & 
Trust in human bureaucrats increases significantly with explainability in high-discretion welfare fraud scenarios. \citep{Grimmelikhuijsen2022} \\ 
& Explainability has a stronger effect depending on the decision context. \citep{Grimmelikhuijsen2022} \\  
& Ref: \citep{Grimmelikhuijsen2022, pasquale_black_2015} \\
